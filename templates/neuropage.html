What is Neuroscience at F&M?

Neuroscience at F&M is offered through the Biological Foundations of Behavior Program. It’s an interdisciplinary major that studies the nervous system from molecules to behavior, drawing on biology, psychology, chemistry, physics, and mathematics.

  • Explore how neural circuits give rise to perception, learning, emotion, and behavior.
  • Build a rigorous foundation in biology, chemistry, physics, math, and psychology before diving into advanced neuroscience.
  • Take cornerstone courses in neuroscience and biopsychology, then customize your path with area-focused electives.
  • Join research labs studying topics like neurobiology, behavior, cognition, hormones and behavior, and brain health.

Electives Info

Neuroscience-focused area electives (sample options):

  • BIO 330 – Advanced Neurobiology
  • BIO 343 – Functional Human Neuroanatomy
  • PSY 357 – Hormones and Behavior with Lab
  • BIO 341 – Neurochemistry

Behavioral & cognitive electives (sample options):

  • PSY 305 – Cognitive Psychology
  • PSY 320 – Animal Behavior with Lab
  • PSY 355 – Face Perception with Lab
  • PSY 371 – Evolutionary Psychology

Related cognate / interdisciplinary options:

  • BIO 306 – Developmental Biology
  • BIO 332 – Molecular Biology
  • PSY 200 – Neuroethics
  • Courses in areas like Philosophy, Anthropology, or Environmental Studies (with approval of the BFB Chair)

Required Classes

Biology Core (3)

  • BIO 101 – Evolution, Ecology and Biodiversity
  • BIO 102 – Cell Biology and Genetics
  • BIO 200 – Integrative Biology

Physical Sciences & Mathematics Core (5)

CHM 111 – General Chemistry I
CHM 112 – General Chemistry II
CHM 211 – Organic Chemistry I

MAT 109 – Calculus I

Fundamentals of Neuroscience (2)

  • BIO 240 – Neuroscience
  • PSY 351 – Biopsychology with Lab or PSY 27x – Biopsychology

Research Methods & Statistics (1)

PSY 300 – Research Design and Statistics
or BIO 210 – Biostatistics

Area Studies Electives (3)

Three courses distributed across at least two areas (e.g., neural & physiological mechanisms, behavioral & cognitive processes, cellular & molecular approaches). At least one must include a lab.

Advanced Research (1)

Take one of the following:

  • BFB 390 – Directed Research
  • BFB 490 – Independent Research
  • Psychology Collaborative Research (PSY 460 or 48x)
  • Approved 300-level Biology laboratory course

(An area studies course may not be double-counted as an advanced research course and vice versa.)
