What is Cognitive Science at F&M?

Click a button to see details.

Cognitive Science at F&M is an interdisciplinary major that explores the mind through multiple perspectives: psychology, neuroscience, computer science, philosophy, and linguistics. Students investigate how we think, learn, perceive, and use language, drawing on scientific methods and computational models to understand cognition.

  • Interdisciplinary approach combining psychology, neuroscience, computer science, philosophy, and linguistics.
  • Hands-on research opportunities in cognitive labs and computational modeling.
  • Explore artificial intelligence, language processing, perception, and decision-making.

While Psychology focuses primarily on human behavior and mental processes, Cognitive Science takes a broader, interdisciplinary approach. We combine insights from psychology, computer science, neuroscience, philosophy, and linguistics to understand cognition in both humans and artificial systems.

No prior programming experience is required. You'll start with CPS 111 (Computer Science I) and build computational skills gradually. Many cognitive science applications use programming for modeling cognition, analyzing data, and creating AI systems.

Students can participate in faculty research labs studying language processing, decision-making, memory, artificial intelligence, and human-computer interaction. Many students present their work at conferences and co-author publications.

Graduates pursue careers in user experience design, artificial intelligence, data science, research, education, and graduate programs in psychology, neuroscience, or computer science. The interdisciplinary training is valued in technology, healthcare, and research industries.
