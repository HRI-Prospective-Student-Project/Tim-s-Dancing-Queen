What is Neuroscience at F&M?

Neuroscience at F&M is part of the Biological Foundations of Behavior (BFB) program. It connects brain, behavior, and mind, from molecules and cells to systems, cognition, and real-world questions.

Click a button to see details.

  • Connects neural mechanisms to behavior, cognition, and mental processes.
  • Progresses from molecules and cells to circuits, systems, and complex behaviors.
  • Emphasizes hands-on lab work and faculty-mentored research across multiple levels of analysis.

Neuroscience is deeply interdisciplinary. You’ll integrate:

  • Biology (neuroanatomy, physiology, cellular & molecular mechanisms).
  • Psychology (behavior, cognition, emotion, learning, and decision-making).
  • Chemistry, physics, and mathematics for quantitative and mechanistic insight.

Research, Outcomes & FAQ

What you’ll do in labs and where students go after F&M.

Research & Labs

Students join labs studying neural systems, hormones, behavior, cognition, and brain health. Many first-years contribute to ongoing projects.

  • Cellular & molecular neuroscience (synapses, neuromodulators, plasticity).
  • Behavior and cognition (learning, attention, decision-making).
  • Hormones and behavior (stress, reproduction, social behavior).

What happens after F&M?

  • Research & Healthcare: lab technicians, clinical research coordinators, and neuroscience-focused roles.
  • Graduate / Professional School: MS/PhD in Neuroscience, Psychology, or Biology; preparation for MD/DO/PA and other health professions.
  • Interdisciplinary Careers: data science, UX research, biotech, science communication, and policy.

How early can I join a lab?

Many students start in their first or second year by talking with faculty and joining research groups or journal clubs.

Is Neuroscience good preparation for medical careers?

Yes. The major overlaps with many pre-health requirements and gives strong training in biology, behavior, and quantitative skills.

Do I need prior neuroscience experience?

No. Introductory biology and psychology courses help you build the foundation you need to succeed in upper-level neuroscience work.
